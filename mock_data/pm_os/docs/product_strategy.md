# Product Strategy — Meridian Commerce

## Thesis
Our growth is shifting from SMB self-serve to **enterprise**. Enterprise buyers need multi-seat purchasing, purchase-order billing, and predictable, auditable checkout. Enterprise Checkout v2 is the keystone of this motion for H1.

## Why now
Sales is increasingly blocked by manual order processing for large accounts. Every enterprise deal that requires a manual invoice adds days to close and burdens finance. Self-serve enterprise checkout removes that friction and is the top revenue lever this half.

## Principles
- **Trust over speed in payments.** A checkout that occasionally double-charges destroys enterprise trust faster than a late feature wins it. We delay before we ship payment risk.
- **Evidence-based launches.** We ship on criteria, not calendar pressure. The launch date serves the criteria, not the reverse.
- **Local-first operations.** Sensitive customer and revenue data stays on-device; our internal tooling (including this PM OS) runs locally.

## Positioning
Enterprise Checkout is how Meridian competes upmarket against incumbents whose enterprise billing is clunky. Reliability and auditability are the differentiators, not feature count.
