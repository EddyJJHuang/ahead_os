# Customer Communications Playbook

Use this playbook when drafting customer-facing or customer-success-facing updates.

## Customer Update Principles

- Acknowledge the blocker plainly.
- Explain the customer impact in their terms.
- Provide the next concrete action and owner.
- Give the next update time.
- Do not blame engineering, QA, legal, or another team.
- Do not promise a ship date unless launch criteria are met.
- Separate what is fixed, what is still being validated, and what is blocked.

## Enterprise Escalation Template

We are holding the launch until {blocker} is resolved and verified. The current evidence is {evidence IDs}. The next step is {owner/action/date}. We will send the next update by {time}.

This keeps the pilot safe because {customer-specific risk}.

## Escalation Rules

For red or critical accounts, include executive sponsor and customer owner. For revenue-at-risk accounts, include the ARR exposure and contract deadline. For payment or data integrity risks, avoid broad rollout until validation is complete.
